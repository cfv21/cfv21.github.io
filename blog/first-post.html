looking forward

in five weeks, i'll graduate from florida state university with a bachelor’s degree in computer science and a minor in mathematics.

it’s a moment i’ve worked toward for years — and like a lot of people in tech, it’s only the beginning. over the last few summers, i’ve been fortunate enough to gain hands-on experience in two very different (but equally technical) internships.

at kingsway community in schenectady, ny, i worked as an it intern, helping monitor, troubleshoot, and improve a real-world network environment. from deploying wireless access points to using tcpdump to debug network issues, it was all about keeping the system running smoothly — and learning a ton in the process.

before that, i spent two summers as a data analyst intern at araya, where i built automated systems to manage file transfers, emails, and storage for thousands of data files. i enjoyed digging into powerShell, SQL, and JSON workflows — but more than anything, i liked the challenge of making something work more efficiently than before.

those experiences made one thing clear: i like solving problems, especially when it involves code, large datasets, and a bit of creative thinking.

my background in math has always pushed me to think critically — to break problems down and rebuild them better. computer science gave that mindset a toolkit. python is my favorite language, but i’m always exploring. whether it's software engineering, data analysis, or something in between, i'm excited to keep building things that are useful and thoughtful.

this site is one of those things. maybe it’s simple right now — but like everything else, it’s only the start.
